About

Dr. Amy Kossoff is a Board Certified Internist and a graduate of the George Washington University School of Medicine. She has over 30 years of experience in Internal Medicine and has worked in homeless shelter-based clinics with underserved populations for the past 23 years. She completed a fellowship at the University of Arizona Center for Integrative Medicine in 2017. She studied hypnosis at the American Society of Clinical Hypnosis in 2016 and 2017 and completed a course on Medical Acupuncture for Physicians at the Helms Medical Institute in May 2018.

Dr. Mary Warfield is a Board Certified Internist, a licensed acupuncturist and a graduate of Georgetown Medical School. She has a masters degree in Clinical Bioethics and over 30 years of experience in healthcare. She spent over 10 years of consultation with Ethics Committees at DC-area hospitals. She began to study acupuncture in 2000 through the University of California, Irvine and subsequently studied Five Element-style acupuncture at the Maryland University of Integrative Health. In May 2018 she completed a course on Medical Acupuncture for Physicians at the Helms Medical Institute.
